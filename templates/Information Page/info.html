What is Gene Editing?

Gene Editing in a Nutshell

Gene editing is a powerful tool used to make highly specific changes to the DNA of a living cell. It allows scientists to cut DNA at precise locations to modify genes.

Gene Therapy History

  • 1970s: Restriction enzymes discovered.
  • 1990s - 2000s: ZFNs and TALENs introduced.
  • 2012: CRISPR-Cas9 revolution.
  • Present: Prime editing and base editing emerging.

CRISPR Overview

CRISPR is a precise gene-editing tool derived from bacterial immune systems. It allows targeted DNA modifications for medicine and agriculture.
